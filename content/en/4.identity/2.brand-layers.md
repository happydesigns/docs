A brand layer adapts presentation on top of reusable UI and product foundations.

Brand layers let products feel specific without changing product behavior. They are mostly configuration and reusable component composition.

A brand layer may change how a product looks and reads. It does not change what the product does.

## Layer scope

A brand layer may provide presentation, defaults, and metadata:

- Tailwind theme tokens.
- Nuxt UI color-role mappings.
- CSS variables.
- Typography defaults.
- Logos and favicons.
- App metadata.
- Layout wrappers.
- Page and frame motifs.
- Brand primitives.
- Component default variants.
- Docs styling.
- Client-specific navigation labels.
- Legal links and footer content.

A brand layer does not provide product behavior or permission to perform real product actions:

- Product or domain logic.
- Authorization or tenant isolation.
- Validation, storage, or data model changes.
- Workflow state changes.
- API, MCP, webhook, or job behavior.
- Production credentials or runtime bindings.
- Audit, compliance, or financial rules.

## Examples

- `happydesigns/brand` can provide the happydesigns wordmark and Nuxt UI color mapping.
- A client identity layer can provide client logos, colors, metadata, and footer links.
- Product workflow changes belong in the product that owns the workflow, not a brand layer.
