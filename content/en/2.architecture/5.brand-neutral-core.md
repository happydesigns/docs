Reusable product cores do not depend on a specific customer or happydesigns visual identity.

Brand-neutral products can run under different brands without changing their code. Identity layers make the product feel specific through tokens, logos, metadata, and app defaults.

Branding changes presentation. Product code owns what the product does.

## For Business OS

- The base product uses Nuxt UI as a calm, efficient operations tool.
- The default experience does not depend on a specific customer brand.
- Branding arrives through Nuxt layers.
- Brand layers theme the product shell. They do not change permissions, workflows, data rules, integrations, storage, or server behavior.

## For `ui`

- `ui` remains a reusable interface foundation.
- Brand-specific color systems, logos, motifs, and visual identity live above it.
- UI defaults may exist, but they must not become a specific brand.

## For identity and brand

Identity layers are mostly configuration. They define tokens, logos, metadata, app config, Nuxt UI defaults, docs styling, and brand primitives. Application logic stays in the product that uses them.
